# Air, Land & Sea: Critters at War — Complete Game Reference

## Overview

Air, Land & Sea is a 2-player card game. Players compete across three theaters of war (Air, Land, Sea) by playing cards to win at least 2 of the 3 theaters each round. The first player to accumulate 12 points wins the game. A key mechanic is the ability to withdraw (concede) a round early to limit how many points the opponent scores.

## Components

- 18 Battle Cards (6 per theater: Air, Land, Sea)
- 2 Supreme Commander cards (one per player, used for first-player tracking and withdrawal scoring)

## Card Anatomy

Each battle card has:
- A **Theater** (Air, Land, or Sea) — determines where it can be played face-up
- A **Strength Value** (1–6)
- A **Tactical Ability** (strength 1–5 cards only; strength 6 cards have no ability)
- Abilities are either **Instant** (trigger once when played face-up or flipped face-up) or **Ongoing** (active as long as the card remains face-up)

## Game Setup

1. Lay the three theater cards in a row between the players. The order is always: Air — Land — Sea (Land is always in the middle; Air and Sea are adjacent to Land but NOT adjacent to each other).
2. Shuffle all 18 battle cards into a single deck.
3. Deal 6 cards to each player (the remaining 6 stay in the deck and are not used this round, keeping hands hidden/uncertain).
4. Determine the first player (use Supreme Commander cards or any method). The first player alternates each round.

## Turn Structure

Players alternate taking turns. On your turn you MUST do one of the following:

1. **Play a card face-up** to its matching theater — use its full strength value and trigger/activate its tactical ability.
2. **Play a card face-down** to ANY theater — it has strength 2 and no ability. Its theater and identity are hidden from the opponent.
3. **Withdraw** — concede the round. Your opponent wins but scores fewer points (see Withdrawal Scoring below).

You play exactly one card per turn (unless an ability grants an extra turn or extra play).

## Playing Cards

### Face-Up
- A card played face-up MUST go to its matching theater (unless an ability like Air Drop or Aerodrome allows otherwise).
- When played face-up, its tactical ability activates:
  - **Instant** abilities resolve immediately and then are done (though the card stays in play for its strength).
  - **Ongoing** abilities remain active as long as the card is face-up. If the card is later flipped face-down, the ongoing ability stops.

### Face-Down
- A card played face-down can go to ANY theater regardless of its printed theater.
- It has strength 2, no ability, and its identity is hidden from the opponent.
- If a face-down card is later flipped face-up, it now uses its printed strength, its ability activates, and it must remain in whatever theater it's in (even if it doesn't match).

### Stacking / Covering
- Cards in a theater are stacked on top of each other (each player has their own stack per theater).
- A card beneath another card is "COVERED." Covered cards still contribute their strength, and their ongoing abilities remain active as long as the card is face-up. A covered card can never be flipped by an ability.
- When you play a new card to a theater, it goes on top of your stack there, covering any previous cards you played to that theater.

## Key Terms

- **FLIP**: Turn a face-up card face-down, or a face-down card face-up. When flipped face-up, instant abilities trigger; ongoing abilities activate. When flipped face-down, ongoing abilities deactivate, strength becomes 2.
- **DISCARD**: Remove the card from play entirely. Place it at the bottom of the deck.
- **MOVE**: Relocate a card from one theater to another. It goes on top of the destination theater stack (same player's side). It keeps its face-up/face-down state.
- **ADJACENT**: Air is adjacent to Land. Sea is adjacent to Land. Air and Sea are NOT adjacent to each other. Land is adjacent to both Air and Sea.

## Winning a Theater

After all cards have been played (or a player withdraws), compare total strength in each theater. Each player sums the strength of all their cards in that theater:
- Face-up cards contribute their printed strength (modified by any active abilities).
- Face-down cards contribute strength 2 (unless modified by an ability like Escalation).
- The player with the higher total strength in a theater wins that theater.
- If tied, the theater is won by the first player (the player who went first this round).

## Winning a Round

The player who wins at least **2 of the 3 theaters** wins the round.

## Scoring

### If a player wins without withdrawal:
The winner scores **6 points**.

### Withdrawal Scoring:
A player may withdraw on their turn instead of playing a card. The opponent wins the round, but scores points based on how many cards the withdrawing player still had in hand. The 1st player gives up fewer points when withdrawing (they have less information since they act first each turn).

| Points | 1st Player (cards remaining) | 2nd Player (cards remaining) |
|--------|------------------------------|------------------------------|
| 2      | 4-6                          | 5-6                          |
| 3      | 2-3                          | 3-4                          |
| 4      | 1                            | 2                            |
| 6      | 0                            | 0-1                          |

## Winning the Game

The first player to reach **12 or more points** wins the game. If both players reach 12+ simultaneously, the player with more points wins. If still tied, play another round.

## End of Round Cleanup

1. Record the score.
2. Collect all 18 battle cards, shuffle, and deal 6 to each player.
3. The first player alternates each round (the player who went second becomes first player).

## Ability Clarifications

- **Support (Air 1)**: Adds +3 to YOUR total strength in EACH adjacent theater (Land gets +3, but NOT Sea since Air is not adjacent to Sea). The +3 is not a card — it's a bonus to your total in those theaters.
- **Air Drop (Air 2)**: The effect lasts for your next turn only. You can play one card face-up to a non-matching theater on that turn.
- **Maneuver (Air 3, Land 3, Sea 3)**: You choose any one card (yours or opponent's) in a theater adjacent to the theater where you played Maneuver, and flip it. This can flip a face-down card face-up (revealing it and activating its ability) or a face-up card face-down (deactivating its ability and reducing it to strength 2).
- **Aerodrome (Air 4)**: As long as this card is face-up, you may play any of your cards with printed strength 3 or less face-up to any theater (ignoring theater matching). This is an ongoing effect.
- **Containment (Air 5)**: As long as face-up, ANY card played facedown by EITHER player is immediately discarded. This is a powerful denial tool.
- **Reinforce (Land 1)**: You look at (but don't reveal) the top card of the deck. You may then play it face-down to a theater adjacent to Land (so Air or Sea). If you choose not to play it, put it back on top.
- **Ambush (Land 2)**: Like Maneuver but can target any theater (not just adjacent ones).
- **Cover Fire (Land 4)**: When played on top of other cards, those covered cards now have strength 4 regardless of their printed strength or face-up/face-down status. Does not affect Cover Fire's own strength (which is 4).
- **Disrupt (Land 5)**: First your opponent must choose one of their own cards and flip it. Then you choose one of your own cards and flip it. You can flip a face-down card face-up to activate its ability.
- **Transport (Sea 1)**: Move one of your cards already in play to a different theater. It keeps its face-up/face-down state and goes on top of your stack in the destination theater.
- **Escalation (Sea 2)**: While face-up, all of your face-down cards everywhere become strength 4 instead of 2.
- **Redeploy (Sea 4)**: Pick up one of your face-down cards from any theater back into your hand. If you do this, you get an extra turn immediately (so you play again).
- **Blockade (Sea 5)**: While face-up, if any card is played to a theater adjacent to Sea (which is only Land) and that theater already has 3 or more cards total (both players' cards combined), the played card is discarded with no effect.

## Strategic Notes

- Withdrawing early is often correct — giving up 2 points is much better than giving up 6.
- Face-down cards are versatile (play anywhere) but weak (only strength 2).
- Flipping opponent's face-down cards face-up can backfire if they placed a strong card there.
- Theater order matters: Land is central and adjacent to both Air and Sea, making it strategically important for adjacency-based abilities.
- The 6 undealt cards create uncertainty — neither player knows the full card pool in play.
